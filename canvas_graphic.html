WHERE DID VIDEO GAMES GET THEIR START?

"A small body of determined spirits fired by an unquenchable faith in their mission can alter the course of history."

 – Mahatma Gandhi

Image Source: http://www.bocamuseum.org/index.php?src=gendocs&ref=ArtofVideoGames&category=Press%20Room

So where did all this begin? The answer is… well, a bit complicated. This is the debate of the century, who’s the father of video games? You can always say Nolan Bushnell because he founded Atari. Perhaps Ralph Baer, back in the late 60’s, early 70’s making the Brown Box? I personally take two different angles here. I can say, you know, whoever started the entire ball rolling is whoever created pong (which is Nolan Bushnell). Whoever started games as we know them today, I’m gonna have to say Shigeru Miyamoto from Nintendo. True video game historians can go all the way back to MIT, the guys who did Spacewar, John Carmack. Most people don’t ever really think about who started gaming, they just appreciate that they did.
